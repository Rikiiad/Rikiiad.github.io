Kokoro Onigiri: A Journey of the Sales Process from Preparation to Daily Bookkeeping

By Riki Adiansyah

Venturing into the Kokoro Onigiri business is not just about creating delicious offerings but also about a systematic and planned process. In this essay, I will explore each stage of the onigiri sales process, starting from the preparation of ingredients and tools to daily bookkeeping on the predetermined schedule.

Selling Kokoro Onigiri is not merely a series of daily tasks but an art that requires attention and dedication. Through ingredient preparation, production, packing, marketing, and bookkeeping, each stage adds a unique touch to the delightful story of Kokoro Onigiri.

1. Ingredient and Tool Preparation (03:45 - 05:00)

Rice is steamed and prepared to perfection, onigiri fillings are arranged neatly, and tools such as spatulas and knives are positioned to ensure quality and efficiency in production.

2. Packing (05:00 - 06:00)

Each onigiri must be carefully packaged to maintain cleanliness and quality during transportation. The use of appropriate packaging also ensures an attractive appearance and consumer appeal.

3. Marketing (06:00)

Onigiri is strategically placed in locations such as BC and SPW, each with 15 pieces. Careful placement maximizes product visibility and facilitates customer access. Marketing involves not only physical placement but also effective promotional strategies.

4. Daily Bookkeeping (16:00)

Every transaction is recorded in detail, including sales and expenses. Daily bookkeeping helps monitor the financial health of the business, identify sales trends, and make strategic decisions for sustainable growth.

Conclusion

The process of selling onigiri is not just a daily routine but a journey that involves skills, dedication, and planning. By following each stage according to the predetermined schedule, an onigiri seller not only creates a quality product but also builds a strong foundation for business growth.
